Ethical AI approach

Technology should support people, not replace responsibility.

Geyvak’s IT branch is built around safe, transparent, practical AI use. AI may draft, organise, summarise, compare, or assist, but important decisions and sensitive outputs should remain human-reviewed.

Clear disclosure

When AI is used in customer-facing work, people should be told in simple language.

Human review

AI outputs involving money, health, care, legal, migration, safety, identity, or public claims should be checked by a person before use.

Privacy first

We collect only what is needed, protect personal details, and avoid unnecessary sensitive data collection.

No fake authority

AI must not pretend to be a lawyer, doctor, accountant, migration agent, registered provider, or lived-experience witness.

No copied identity

Creative AI should support original expression, not copy another person’s voice, likeness, work, or protected identity.

Practical usefulness

We avoid overcomplicated systems. The best technology is understandable, maintainable, and genuinely useful.
