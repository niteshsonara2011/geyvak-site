Bhavaઈ Theatre Art begins here.

Bhavaઈ Theatre Art is Geyvak's living performance art branch, rooted in Gujarati Bhavai folk theatre, movement, music, storytelling, humour, devotion, costume transformation, and public performance.

Bhavaઈ is performed not only through dance, rhythm, costume, and character, but emotionally — through Bhava: feeling, expression, connection, memory, satire, courage, and the lived experiences of people.

This branch begins softly in Melbourne / Naarm through introduction posters, character development, transformable costume design, artist collaboration, public storytelling, busking preparation, workshops, and future cultural performance programs.

Character rooms

Three character directions

The gallery introduces the transformable costume idea through three strong identities: story carrier, playful witness, and inner devotional presence.

Sutradhar / story carrier

Earthy, grounded, and dignified. The host-character who carries the audience into the world of Bhavaઈ.

  • Angrakha layer
  • Shoulder drape
  • Mirror-work border
  • Small pagdi / turban language

Ranglo / playful witness

Public-facing, humorous, sharp, and full of rhythm. A character of satire, joy, social reflection, and connection.

  • Patchwork vest
  • Tassels and bells
  • Waist sash
  • Vibrant mixed palette

Bhavaઈ Devi / inner devotional presence

Mystical, powerful, and commanding. A ceremonial presence carried through shoulder drape, hand drape, ornament, rhythm, and emotional force.

  • Ceremonial shoulder and hand drape
  • Detachable skirt panels
  • Statement bangles
  • Deep regal palette
